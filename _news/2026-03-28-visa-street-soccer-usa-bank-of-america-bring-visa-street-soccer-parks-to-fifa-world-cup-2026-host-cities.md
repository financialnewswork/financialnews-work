With 75 days until the FIFA World Cup 2026, Visa, Street Soccer USA, and Bank of America have announced the expansion of Visa Street Soccer Parks to all 11 U.S. host cities. The initiative transforms underutilized spaces into community hubs featuring two professional-grade fields, lighting, learning centers, and flexible gathering spaces.

New parks will open in Boston, Dallas/Fort Worth, Houston, Los Angeles, Miami, Philadelphia, and Tacoma, joining existing installations in San Francisco, Denver, Kansas City, and New York City. Programming at each location includes youth and adult leagues, academic support, financial education, and job readiness training delivered through local partnerships.

The effort aims to create lasting community infrastructure that will outlive the tournament itself, providing ongoing venues for sports, learning, and connection in underserved neighborhoods.
