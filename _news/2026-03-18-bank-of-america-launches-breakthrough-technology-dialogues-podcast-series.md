Bank of America has launched the Breakthrough Technology Dialogues podcast series, a new audio platform showcasing emerging technologies reshaping the global economy. The series covers artificial intelligence, quantum computing, fusion energy, medtech, and robotics through focused conversations with technology leaders.

Each episode runs precisely 19 minutes and 56 seconds, a reference to when the term "byte" was first coined. Guests are drawn from attendees of the annual Breakthrough Technology Dialogue events held in the UK and Singapore. Bernard Mensah, President of International at Bank of America, said the series aims to "bring trusted insight to a rapidly changing landscape."

The inaugural episode features Hermann Hauser, ARM founder and prominent technology investor, discussing quantum error correction and its implications for AI, synthetic biology, and computing. James Harding, Editor-in-Chief of The Observer, serves as host. The podcast is available on PodBean and major podcast platforms.
